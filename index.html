01 ¬∑ Fake messages & links

Fraudsters copy bank logos, UPI apps and government schemes, then send WhatsApp/SMS links asking you to ‚Äúverify‚Äù details or approve a small refund.

Real banks ask you to open the official app yourself. Never share OTPs or click payment links sent on chat.

02 ¬∑ Investment & job scams

"Daily profit / part‚Äëtime task earning"

Fake trading platforms, Telegram groups and task‚Äëbased jobs lure adults with screenshots of huge returns, then slowly push them to deposit more money.

03 ¬∑ Data leaks & identity theft

When databases or devices are compromised, PAN, Aadhaar and phone numbers can be abused to open accounts or take instant loans without your knowledge.

Watch SMS/email for new loan, SIM or card messages you did not request and contact the bank/provider quickly.

Four habits that quietly protect you

Think of these like brushing your teeth for the internet: small routines that keep bigger problems away.

1
Lock your main accounts first

Start with email, social media, work accounts and banking. Use strong, unique passwords and turn on 2‚Äëstep verification (2FA) for each one.

2
Let updates protect you

Allow automatic updates on your phone, browser and operating system. They close security holes that attackers reuse across thousands of victims.

3

If a message feels urgent, emotional or ‚Äútoo good‚Äù, slow down. For money or KYC issues, go to the official app or website yourself instead of links.

4

Keep important photos and documents in at least two places (like your device plus a cloud backup). Even if one device is hit, your memories and records stay safe.

If something feels wrong online

Maybe your account posted something you didn't, or your phone is acting strange. Don't freeze or feel embarrassed ‚Äî use this checklist.

1 ¬∑ Change passwords from a safe device.
If your phone or laptop might be infected, borrow a trusted device first. Change the password for the affected account, then your email and banking.
2 ¬∑ Turn on 2‚Äëstep verification.
Add 2‚Äëstep verification to your main accounts and remove unknown devices from the list of logged‚Äëin sessions.
3 ¬∑ Scan and clean your devices.
Run a full security scan, uninstall strange apps or extensions, and install any pending updates.
4 ¬∑ Talk to your bank or app support.
For unknown payments or posts, contact official bank helplines or app support pages (not phone numbers from random messages).
5 ¬∑ Report serious cases to cybercrime portals.
Keep screenshots, IDs and messages. Use ‚öñÔ∏è cybercrime.gov.in ¬∑ 1930 for serious fraud or blackmail in India.
Good to remember: scams work by making you feel rushed, stupid or alone. Taking a breath, asking someone you trust, and following a simple plan is exactly how professionals handle incidents too.

You can even set a small ‚Äúdigital checkup‚Äù once a month: quickly review logins, 2‚Äëstep verification, app permissions and bank alerts. It keeps you in control without needing to be a hacker.
